Beef in Black Bean Sauce

Description

Beef in Black Bean Sauce is a flavorful and hearty stir-fry dish that combines tender marinated beef with vibrant vegetables and a rich black bean sauce. Perfect for serving over rice or couscous, it’s a dish bursting with bold flavors and textures.

Ingredients

For the Beef Marinade (Serves 2)

  • 350 g beef slices
  • 2 tsp soy sauce
  • 1/2 tsp salt (or to taste)
  • 1/2 tsp baking soda
  • 1 tsp grated garlic
  • 1 tsp grated ginger
  • 1 tsp cornstarch
  • 1/2 bottle black bean marinade

For the Stir-Fry

  • 2.5 tbsp oil
  • The marinated beef
  • 10 cloves garlic, diced
  • 1.5 tbsp Chinese fermented black beans (optional)
  • 1/2 medium onion, chopped
  • 1 red bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 green bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 medium red chili, chopped
  • 2 ounces sliced carrot
  • Optional: 1 tbsp cornflour mixed with a little cold water (to thicken the sauce if needed)
  • 1 tbsp dried parsley (for garnish)

For the Couscous (Optional)

  • 100 g couscous
  • 125 ml boiling water
  • 1 tbsp butter

Steps

    Marinating the Beef

  1. In a bowl, combine soy sauce, salt, baking soda, grated garlic, grated ginger, cornstarch, and half a bottle of black bean marinade.
  2. Add the beef slices to the marinade and mix well. Let it sit for at least 20-30 minutes to absorb the flavors.

    3. Preparing the Stir-Fry

  3. Heat 2.5 tbsp of oil in a large frying pan or wok over medium heat.
  4. Add the marinated beef and stir-fry until browned and nearly cooked through. Remove from the pan and set aside.
  5. In the same pan, sauté the diced garlic until fragrant.
  6. If using, add the Chinese fermented black beans and stir for 30 seconds.
  7. Add the onion, bell peppers, red chili, and sliced carrot. Stir-fry for 3-4 minutes until the vegetables are slightly tender but still crisp.
  8. Return the beef to the pan and mix well with the vegetables.
  9. If the sauce appears too thin, mix 1 tbsp of cornflour with a little cold water, then add to the stir-fry. Cook for an additional 1-2 minutes until the sauce thickens.
  10. Top with dried parsley before serving.

    11. Preparing the Couscous (Optional)

  11. Place the couscous in a small saucepan and pour boiling water over it.
  12. Cover and let sit for 2-5 minutes until the water is fully absorbed and the couscous is fluffy.
  13. Add 1 tbsp of butter and mix over low heat until the butter is melted and well incorporated.
  14. Serve alongside the beef stir-fry for a complete meal.
