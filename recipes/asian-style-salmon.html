Asian Style Salmon

Description

Asian Style Salmon is a flavorful and satisfying dish that combines the richness of salmon with a sweet and spicy Asian-inspired sauce. Itâ€™s perfect as a main course, paired with optional vegetables or couscous for a wholesome meal.

Ingredients

For the Salmon

  • 2 salmon fillets
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 2 red chilies, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional: 1/2 tsp garlic, minced

For the Asian Sauce

  • 1 tsp Lee Kum Kee Teriyaki sauce
  • 1 tsp Sriracha
  • 1 tbsp honey
  • 1/4 breakfast bowl soy sauce
  • Little dark soy sauce (for deeper flavor)

Optional Vegetable Toppings

  • 1 tbsp sesame seeds
  • 2 tbsp chopped celery
  • 2 tbsp chopped spring onions
  • Julienned carrots

For the Couscous (Optional)

  • 100 g couscous
  • 125 ml boiling water
  • 1 tbsp butter

Steps

    Preparing the Salmon

  1. Heat sesame oil in a medium-large frying pan over medium heat.
  2. Place the salmon fillets in the pan and sprinkle with chopped red chilies, salt, and pepper. Add garlic if desired.
  3. Cook the salmon until golden brown on the outside and cooked through, about 4-5 minutes per side.
  4. Remove the salmon from the pan and set aside. Discard excess oil from the pan.
  5. On a separate plate, shred or chop the cooked salmon into bite-sized pieces.

    6. Making the Sauce

  6. In a bowl, mix the teriyaki sauce, Sriracha, honey, soy sauce, and dark soy sauce.
  7. Return the shredded salmon to the pan on low heat and pour the sauce mixture over it.
  8. Mix well and cook for 2-3 minutes, ensuring the salmon is evenly coated with the sauce.

    9. Optional Vegetable Toppings

  9. Add sesame seeds, chopped celery, spring onions, or julienned carrots as garnish or toppings.

    10. Preparing the Couscous (Optional)

  10. Place couscous in a small saucepan and pour boiling water over it.
  11. Cover and let sit for 2-5 minutes until the water is fully absorbed and the couscous is fluffy.
  12. Add 1 tbsp of butter and mix over low heat until the butter is melted and well incorporated.
  13. Serve the salmon with the couscous on the side.
